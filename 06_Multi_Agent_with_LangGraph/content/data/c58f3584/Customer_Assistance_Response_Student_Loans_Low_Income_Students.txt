**Customer Assistance Response: Positioning of Student Loans for Low-Income Students**

Thank you for reaching out to us with your inquiry regarding the impact of student loans on low-income students. We want to acknowledge that this is a significant concern, especially in today’s educational and economic climate.

Recent data reveals the ongoing challenges faced by low-income students when it comes to financing their education. As of 2023, a considerable percentage of student loan borrowers have outstanding debts under $25,000, with many remaining in forbearance due to the economic disruptions caused by the pandemic. The resumption of federal student loan payments in October 2023 has further highlighted the financial strain on borrowers—particularly those from low-income backgrounds. Many of these students may feel overwhelmed, as they lack the financial security and resources necessary to manage debt effectively, which often increases their risk of financial distress and affects their long-term career prospects.

Currently, it is concerning to note that around 31% of lower-income borrowers are unaware of the various repayment plans available to them, which could provide some relief from their financial burden. The new, scaled-down SAVE plan, introduced in August 2023, is designed to assist borrowers by allowing monthly payments to be reduced by as much as half, with loan balances forgiven after ten or twenty years based on income level. Such options can significantly alleviate some of the pressures faced by low-income borrowers.

Furthermore, we recognize that low-income and first-generation college students often belong to marginalized communities, facing higher dropout rates, which creates an ongoing cycle of financial hardship that raises serious equity concerns in educational access. Many of these students do not have the generational wealth necessary to support their educational pursuits, complicating their ability to repay loans and exacerbating their financial situation.

In response to these challenges, we are wholeheartedly committed to providing the necessary resources and support to guide students as they navigate their financial journeys. We encourage students to explore grants, scholarships, and financial aid options, which can significantly minimize their reliance on loans and help alleviate their overall financial burden.

Please remember, if you have any further questions or need assistance, we are here for you every step of the way on your educational journey. Your concerns matter to us, and we are dedicated to helping you find the best path forward.

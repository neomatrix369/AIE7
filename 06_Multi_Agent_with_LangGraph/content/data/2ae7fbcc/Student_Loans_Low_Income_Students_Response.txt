### Overview of Student Loans for Low-Income Students

Student loans play a vital role in fulfilling the dreams of low-income students who aspire to achieve higher education. However, we understand that managing the financial responsibilities that come with these loans requires careful navigation and planning.

1. **Income-Based Repayment Plans (IBR)**: These compassionate repayment plans are designed to accommodate borrowers facing financial challenges. Payments are tailored to your income, ensuring they are more manageable. For individuals who decide to pursue careers in public service, there is a possibility that remaining loan balances can be forgiven after 20 to 25 years of qualifying payments. It's important to note, however, that forgiven amounts may be treated as taxable income, which can have future financial implications.

2. **Subsidized Loans**: Many low-income students have the opportunity to benefit from subsidized loans. These loans do not accrue interest while you are enrolled at least half-time, helping to ease the burden of repayment later on and ultimately reducing the total financial strain.

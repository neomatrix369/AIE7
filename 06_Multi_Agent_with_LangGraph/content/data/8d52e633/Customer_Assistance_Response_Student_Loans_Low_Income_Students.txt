### Overview of Student Loans for Low-Income Students:

1. **Types of Loans**: Low-income students typically qualify for federal subsidized loans, specifically designed for those demonstrating financial need. These loans do not accumulate interest while the student is enrolled at least half-time in college, making them a favorable option.

2. **Federal Direct Subsidized and Unsubsidized Loans**:
   - **Subsidized Loans**: Available to students with financial need, these loans do not accrue interest while the student is in school.
   - **Unsubsidized Loans**: These are available regardless of need, but interest starts accruing immediately. Both types have annual and aggregate loan limits that vary by dependency status and year in school.

3. **Income-Driven Repayment Plans**: For graduates navigating repayment, income-based repayment plans can be particularly helpful. These plans adjust monthly payments based on income and family size, making it easier for lower-income borrowers to manage loan obligations without compromising their financial stability.

4. **Loan Forgiveness Programs**: There are several programs aimed at supporting low-income students, including the Public Service Loan Forgiveness program, which benefits borrowers who pursue careers in public service and non-profit work.

5. **Additional Resources**:  
   - The FAFSA (Free Application for Federal Student Aid) is essential as it allows low-income students to access federal loans and grants. It is generally available starting October 1st for the following academic year, but early submission is encouraged to maximize aid opportunities.
   - For personalized assistance, you may want to consult with your school’s financial aid office, which can provide tailored advice based on your specific situation.
